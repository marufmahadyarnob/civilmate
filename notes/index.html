Concrete & Mix Design

Dry Volume Factor
Dry Vol = Wet Vol × 1.54
Cement, sand and aggregate in dry state occupy ~54% more volume due to voids and compaction.
Cement Quantity (M20 – 1:1.5:3)
Cement = (1/5.5) × Dry Vol × 1440 / 50 bags
Total ratio = 1+1.5+3 = 5.5. Density of cement ≈ 1440 kg/m³. 1 bag = 50 kg.
Water-Cement Ratio
W/C = Water (kg) / Cement (kg)
Lower W/C → higher strength. For M20: W/C = 0.45–0.55.
Compressive Strength (IS 456)
fck = Characteristic compressive strength at 28 days
M20 → fck = 20 MPa. Design strength fd = 0.67 × fck / γm.

Soil Mechanics

Void Ratio
e = Vv / Vs
Volume of voids divided by volume of solids. Ranges 0.3–3 for soils.
Porosity
n = e / (1 + e) = Vv / V
Fraction of total volume occupied by voids. n ranges 0–1.
Dry Unit Weight
γd = Gs·γw / (1 + e)
Unit weight of soil with no water content.
Terzaghi's Bearing Capacity
qu = c·Nc + q·Nq + 0.5·γ·B·Nγ
Ultimate bearing capacity for shallow strip footings. Nc, Nq, Nγ are bearing capacity factors.
Darcy's Law
v = k · i
Seepage velocity v = coefficient of permeability k × hydraulic gradient i.

Structural Engineering

Bending Moment (Simply Supported, UDL)
M = w·L² / 8
Maximum bending moment at midspan. w = UDL (kN/m), L = span (m).
Shear Force (Simply Supported)
V = w·L / 2
Maximum shear force at supports for a uniformly distributed load.
Euler's Column Buckling
Pcr = π²·E·I / (Le)²
Critical load for slender column. Le = effective length. E = modulus of elasticity.
Section Modulus
Z = I / y
I = moment of inertia, y = distance from NA. For rectangular: Z = bd²/6.
Bending Stress
σ = M / Z = M·y / I
Flexural stress at a fibre located at distance y from the neutral axis.

Hydraulics & Fluid Mechanics

Bernoulli's Equation
P/γ + v²/2g + z = constant
Sum of pressure head, velocity head and datum head is constant along a streamline.
Manning's Equation
V = (1/n) · R^(2/3) · S^(1/2)
Open channel flow velocity. n = Manning's roughness, R = hydraulic radius, S = slope.
Continuity Equation
A₁V₁ = A₂V₂ = Q
Conservation of mass for incompressible flow. Q = discharge (m³/s).

Transportation Engineering

Stopping Sight Distance
SSD = v·t + v²/(2·g·f)
v = design speed (m/s), t = reaction time (2.5s), f = friction coefficient.
CBR (California Bearing Ratio)
CBR = (Test load / Standard load) × 100
Used to design flexible pavement thickness. Higher CBR → thinner pavement needed.
